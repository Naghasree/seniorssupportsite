Comparison Guide

How to Compare Assisted Living Options

Choosing assisted living is easier when families compare communities using the same criteria. Focus on care level, staffing, transparency, and total monthly cost.

Care Level and Medical Support

Ask what level of personal care is included and what requires extra fees. Confirm whether memory care support is available now or later if needs change.

Staffing and Training

Review staff-to-resident ratios, overnight coverage, and caregiver training. Consistent staffing can impact safety, communication, and resident well-being.

Cost Structure

Look beyond base rent. Ask for a full fee breakdown: medication management, care add-ons, transportation, and annual rate increase policy.

Environment and Daily Life

Tour common areas, dining, and activity spaces. Ask residents and families about responsiveness, cleanliness, and communication during concerns.

Comparison Checklist

  • Create a side-by-side cost sheet for 3 communities
  • Confirm licensing and inspection history
  • Ask for sample monthly statements
  • Review move-in and move-out policies
  • Document your top priorities before final decision
