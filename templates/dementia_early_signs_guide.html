Early Signs of Dementia: Family Guide

Families often notice subtle changes before a diagnosis. Recognizing early patterns can help you seek evaluation sooner and prepare the right support plan.

Common Early Changes

Watch for repeated questions, confusion with time or place, difficulty following familiar steps, and unusual judgment changes. One symptom alone does not confirm dementia.

What to Document

Keep examples with dates and context. A clear record helps doctors evaluate changes more accurately than general memory concerns.

When to Seek Medical Evaluation

Schedule an appointment when cognitive changes affect daily safety, medications, finances, or communication. Early evaluation improves planning options.

Family Communication

Use calm, supportive language and avoid arguments about details. Focus on safety, dignity, and practical routines while medical assessment is in progress.

Early Planning Checklist

  • Track memory and behavior changes with dates
  • Book cognitive evaluation appointment
  • Review medication list and possible interactions
  • Discuss legal and care planning documents early
  • Create a safe daily routine at home
