Safety Guide

Home Safety Checklist for Older Adults

A safer home reduces fall risk and helps older adults stay independent longer. Start with the rooms used every day and make improvements one area at a time.

Entryways and Hallways

Make sure walkways are uncluttered, well lit, and easy to access. Remove loose rugs, secure cords, and add stable handrails where needed.

Bathroom Safety

The bathroom is one of the highest-risk rooms. Consider grab bars, non-slip mats, a raised toilet seat, and brighter lighting around the sink and shower.

Bedroom and Night Access

Keep a lamp within reach of the bed and create a clear path to the bathroom. Nightlights can make a major difference during late-night trips.

Kitchen and Daily Living Areas

Store common items at waist height to reduce climbing and bending. Use chairs with strong armrests and make sure emergency phone numbers are easy to find.

Quick Safety Checklist

  • Remove loose rugs and clutter
  • Install brighter lighting and nightlights
  • Add grab bars in the bathroom
  • Keep emergency contacts visible
  • Check smoke alarms and carbon monoxide detectors
