Medicare Basics for Families in 2026

If you are helping a parent or older adult understand Medicare, start with the four main parts and the enrollment timeline. The goal is to compare coverage clearly before deadlines pass.

Understand the Four Main Parts

Part A typically covers hospital care. Part B covers doctor visits and outpatient care. Part D helps with prescription coverage. Part C, often called Medicare Advantage, combines benefits through private plans.

Know the Enrollment Windows

Missing the first enrollment period can lead to delays or penalties. Families should review eligibility, current employer coverage, and prescription needs before choosing a plan.

Compare Costs, Not Just Premiums

Look at deductibles, copays, prescriptions, provider networks, and yearly out-of-pocket limits. A plan with a lower premium may still cost more overall depending on medical needs.

Simple Family Checklist

  • List current doctors and medications
  • Write down current insurance coverage
  • Compare at least two plan options
  • Check enrollment deadlines
  • Save plan confirmation details in one folder
