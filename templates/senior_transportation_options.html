Mobility Guide

Transportation Options for Seniors

Reliable transportation is essential for medical appointments, grocery trips, and social connection. Families should compare options by cost, accessibility, and reliability.

Paratransit Services

Many local transit systems offer door-to-door or curb-to-curb services for qualified riders. Booking rules and eligibility vary by county.

Non-Emergency Medical Transport

For medical visits, non-emergency transportation may be available through insurance plans, local agencies, or specialized private providers.

Rideshare and Taxi Options

Rideshare can work well when a caregiver can schedule and monitor rides. Confirm pickup support, waiting policies, and accessibility needs.

Volunteer and Community Programs

Faith groups, senior centers, and nonprofit organizations sometimes provide volunteer ride programs with lower costs.

Transportation Planning Checklist

  • List recurring monthly trips
  • Compare cost and reliability for each option
  • Ask about wheelchair or mobility support
  • Save backup transportation contacts
  • Book medical transport in advance when possible
